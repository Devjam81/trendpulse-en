Caroline Guiela Nguyen: The Visionary Architect of a Multilingual and Heartfelt Theater

A major figure on the international stage, Caroline Guiela Nguyen is redefining contemporary creation by placing life stories and cultural diversity at the core of her work. As the current director of the Théâtre National de Strasbourg, she weaves a powerful connection between intimate narratives and the grand scale of history.

A Journey Rooted in Plural Identity

Born to a Vietnamese mother and an Algerian father, Caroline Guiela Nguyen carries within her a crossroads of cultures that naturally infuses her artistic vision. After studying performing arts and attending the prestigious school of the Théâtre National de Strasbourg (TNS), she founded her company, Les Hommes Approximatifs, in 2009. Her background isn't just a biographical detail; it is the engine of her creative process.

Her rise was lightning-fast. In just a few years, she transitioned from local scenes to the official selection of the Avignon Festival, establishing herself as a voice that refuses to simplify the world. She seeks to show what she calls "the missing bodies" of the stage—those people, languages, and stories that traditionally remain in the wings of historical narratives.

The Art of "Hyper-Storytelling": A Unique Creative Method

What makes Nguyen’s work unmistakable is her commitment to "reality-based" fiction. She doesn't just write plays; she conducts long-term investigative work. To build a show, her team immerses itself in specific environments—be it a Vietnamese restaurant, a prison, or an upholstery workshop—to capture the truth of gestures and speech.
  • Multilingualism: Characters speak French, Vietnamese, Arabic, or English, reflecting the reality of our globalized world.
  • A "Film-Like" Aesthetic: Her productions often feature hyper-realistic sets (designed by Alice Duchange) that allow for cinematic storytelling on stage.
  • The Power of Emotion: Unlike purely intellectual theater, her work aims straight for the heart, using tears and empathy as bridges between the audience and the performers.

Landmark Creations: From "Saigon" to "Lacrima"

Any portrait of Caroline Guiela Nguyen must highlight the masterpieces that have toured the globe.

1. "Saigon" (2017): This epic saga, set in a restaurant across two timelines (1956 and 1996), explores the wounds of exile and the French-Vietnamese relationship. It remains her most iconic success to date. 2. "Fraternité, Conte fantastique" (2021): A shift toward science fiction to explore loss and the resilience of human bonds. 3. "Lacrima" (2024): Her latest ambitious project, which follows the creation of a royal wedding dress between Alençon, Mumbai, and Casablanca. This play highlights the "invisible hands" of craftsmanship and the weight of secrets hidden in the seams of history.

Through these works, she demonstrates that the most specific, local stories are often the ones that achieve universal resonance.

Leading the TNS: A New Breath for French Institutions

In 2023, Caroline Guiela Nguyen made history by becoming the first woman of color to lead the Théâtre National de Strasbourg. Her appointment marks a turning point for French cultural institutions. Under her leadership, the TNS is evolving into an "open house," focused on professional integration and diversity.

She advocates for a "sustainable theater," where production time is respected and where the school and the stage nourish each other. Her ambition is clear: to ensure that the theater of tomorrow looks like the society outside its doors. By bridging the gap between high art and popular emotion, she proves that contemporary theater can be both demanding and profoundly accessible.
