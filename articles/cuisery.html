Cuisery: An Immersive Journey into the Heart of the Village of Books and Burgundian Heritage

Nestled in the rolling greenery of Southern Burgundy, Cuisery stands as a vibrant sanctuary where history meets the timeless magic of the printed word. This hidden gem invites travelers to wander through medieval streets where every corner tells a story, blending architectural grandeur with a passion for antique literature.

A Literary Renaissance: The Village of Books Since 1999

Cuisery joined the prestigious circle of "Villages du Livre" in 1999, transforming this historic market town into a paradise for bibliophiles. Today, it ranks among the most significant book towns in Europe, attracting collectors and curious visitors alike to its specialized boutiques.

The experience of walking through Cuisery is defined by:

  • Antique Bookshops: Over a dozen professional booksellers house rare editions, vintage comics, and out-of-print treasures.
  • Artisan Workshops: Visitors can observe traditional crafts, including bookbinding, calligraphy, and professional printing techniques.
  • The Gutenberg Space: A dedicated cultural center that hosts workshops and exhibitions, keeping the history of printing alive for younger generations.
  • The Monthly Book Market: Every first Sunday of the month, the village comes alive with a massive open-air market dedicated to paper and literature.

Architectural Wonders: Between Castles and Churches

Beyond its literary fame, Cuisery is a showcase of Burgundian architectural evolution. The village’s layout still reflects its medieval importance as a defensive stronghold and a religious hub.

The centerpiece of the village is the Church of Notre-Dame de l'Assomption, built in the 16th century. It is famous for its "Poutre de Gloire" (Rood Screen) and its exceptional acoustics. Nearby, the remnants of the ancient fortifications and the "Tour de l'Horloge" (Clock Tower) offer panoramic views of the Seille valley, reminding visitors of Cuisery’s strategic past as a border town between France and the Holy Roman Empire.

Nature and Science: The Eden Center and the Seille River

Cuisery is not just about the past; it is also a place that looks toward the natural world and the stars. The Eden Center is a must-visit regional museum dedicated to biodiversity. Through interactive exhibits, it explores the local flora and fauna of the Burgundy region, making it an ideal stop for families.

The village is also defined by its proximity to the Seille, one of the most beautiful navigable rivers in France. This quiet waterway provides:

  • Boating excursions: Gentle cruises to discover the river’s ecosystem.
  • Fishing spots: Renowned for their tranquility and abundance.
  • Hiking trails: Paths that follow the riverbanks, offering a perfect blend of exercise and scenic beauty.

Gastronomy and Living History

An immersion into Burgundian heritage would be incomplete without savoring the local terroir. Cuisery is located at the crossroads of some of France's most prestigious culinary regions: Bresse to the south and the Burgundy vineyards to the west.

Visitors can enjoy local specialties such as Bresse poultry (the only one with an AOP labels), refined cheeses like Comte or Epoisses, and world-class wines from the nearby Côte chalonnaise. The village’s restaurants often occupy historic buildings, providing an atmosphere where the stone walls and wooden beams add as much flavor to the meal as the local produce itself.

Conclusion Cuisery is a rare destination that nourishes both the mind and the senses, offering a perfect balance between cultural depth and natural serenity. Whether you are a dedicated book collector, a history enthusiast, or simply a traveler seeking the authentic soul of Burgundy, this village promises a profound and memorable escape.

Plan your visit today: Stop by Cuisery during the next monthly book market to experience the true essence of this literary capital in the heart of the French countryside.
