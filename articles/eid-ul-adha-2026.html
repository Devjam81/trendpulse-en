The Significance and Timing of Éid Ul Adha 2026

Rituals and Traditions: The Act of Qurbani

The heart of the celebration lies in the ritual of Qurbani (sacrifice). This tradition is not merely symbolic; it is a practical application of social justice and wealth redistribution.

  • The Sacrifice: Families who have the financial means sacrifice an animal (usually a sheep, goat, cow, or camel).
  • The Three-Way Split: Maintaining the spirit of equity, the meat is traditionally divided into three equal parts: one for the family, one for relatives and friends, and one for the underprivileged.
The Takbir: From the dawn of the Day of Arafah until the final days of Eid, the air is filled with the Takbir*, a rhythmic chant praising the greatness of God. Eid Prayer: The day begins with a special congregational prayer (Salat al-Eid*) held in large open fields or mosques, fostering a sense of immense brotherhood.
