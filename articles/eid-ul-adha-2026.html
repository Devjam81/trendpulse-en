The Significance and Lunar Calendar of 2026

Rituals and the Spirit of Qurbani

The hallmark of Éid Ul Adha is the Qurbani (sacrifice). This ritual is not merely a formality but a structured act of charity designed to foster social equity. In 2026, as global awareness of food security grows, the distribution of meat remains a critical pillar of the celebration.

  • The Prayer (Salat al-Eid): The day begins with a special congregational prayer held in large open fields or mosques.
  • The Sacrifice: After the prayer, those who are financially able sacrifice a livestock animal (sheep, goat, cow, or camel).
  • The Rule of Three: The meat is traditionally divided into three equal parts:
* One-third for the immediate family. * One-third for relatives and friends. * One-third for the poor and vulnerable members of society.
