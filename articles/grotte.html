The Caves: Secret Portals to the Depths of the Earth

Caves are magical places that fascinate and unsettle at the same time. As if the gates of hell had opened, revealing secrets forgotten for millions of years. And yet, these natural caverns invite us to explore the depths of the Earth, to discover the wonders that lie in the darkness.

The Caves: Natural Formations

Caves are natural formations created by the erosion of rocks and minerals over time. Water is often the main actor in this process, which can take millions of years. Caves can be formed by the melting of glaciers, rivers, or even the action of winds. They come in all sizes, from small crevices to gigantic caverns.

The Diversity of Caves

Caves are an example of exceptional biodiversity. The specific conditions of these places create unique ecosystems that host species adapted to these dark and silent environments. You can find species of fish, insects, and birds that have evolved to thrive in these conditions.
