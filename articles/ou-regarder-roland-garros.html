Roland-Garros: The Ultimate Guide to Watching Every Match Live and Legal

The clay-court season reaches its breathtaking climax at Porte d’Auteuil, where the world’s elite battle for the Musketeers' Cup. Whether you are rooting for the next generation of stars or seasoned legends, knowing exactly where to tune in is the first step to not missing a single drop of sweat on the red dirt.

The Official Broadcasting Landscape: Who Holds the Rights?

Television rights for Roland-Garros are strictly regulated to ensure high-quality coverage across different regions. In France, the historical home of the tournament, the broadcasting landscape is split between public service and premium streaming. France Télévisions remains the bedrock of free-to-air coverage, while Amazon Prime Video has secured exclusive rights to specific sessions.

Internationally, the distribution varies significantly. In the United States, NBC Sports and Peacock handle the major rounds, while Eurosport remains the dominant force across most of Europe. Understanding these shifts is crucial because prime-time "Night Sessions"—often featuring the most high-stakes matchups—are frequently locked behind specific digital subscriptions rather than traditional cable channels.

Where to Watch for Free: Public Broadcasters and Options

For fans who want to enjoy the matches without an expensive subscription, several options exist depending on your geographical location. Public broadcasters often prioritize the main courts (Philippe-Chatrier and Suzanne-Lenglen) and matches involving homegrown athletes.

  • France Télévisions (France): Offers comprehensive coverage on France 2, France 3, and France 4. Their digital platform, France.tv, allows you to toggle between multiple courts simultaneously.
  • RTBF & VRT (Belgium): These public channels provide excellent French and Dutch language coverage for local residents.
  • SRG SSR (Switzerland): A reliable source for Swiss fans, especially during deep runs by national favorites.
  • International Highlights: The official Roland-Garros YouTube channel provides daily recaps, "Point of the Day" features, and post-match interviews accessible globally for free.

Premium Streaming and Exclusive Night Sessions

The introduction of Night Sessions has revolutionized how fans consume the French Open. These high-octane matches, played under the lights of the renovated Philippe-Chatrier court, often require a specific digital subscription.

Amazon Prime Video is currently the exclusive host for these evening thrills in France, including all matches played on the Simonne-Mathieu court. For a small monthly fee, subscribers gain access to expert commentary and high-definition streams that can be paused or rewound.

In other territories, Eurosport and the discovery+ app offer an "all-courts" pass. This is arguably the best option for hardcore tennis enthusiasts who want to follow a specific doubles pair or a junior player early in the tournament, as every single televised court is available to stream live without interruption.

How to Follow the Action on the Go: Digital Tools

If you cannot be in front of a TV, the digital ecosystem of Roland-Garros ensures you remain updated in real-time. Modern fans utilize a mix of official apps and social media to stay in the loop.

  • The Official Roland-Garros App: Available on iOS and Android, it provides live scores, real-time statistics, and radio commentary which is perfect for commuting.
  • Push Notifications: Enable alerts for your favorite players to get instant updates on set scores and match points.
  • Social Media Hubs: Follow the tournament's official X (formerly Twitter) and Instagram accounts for behind-the-scenes footage and immediate clips of incredible rallies.
  • Interactive Brackets: Many platforms now offer "live brackets" that update immediately as a match ends, helping you visualize the path to the final.

Conclusion: Get Ready for the Red Clay Drama

Navigating the broadcast maze of Roland-Garros doesn't have to be complicated. Whether you choose the reliability of public television or the exhaustive coverage of premium streaming apps, your viewing experience will be defined by the unparalleled intensity of clay-court tennis.
